A note before we start

Stop making cruise commercials.
Start making the room that’s already in their head.

Your AI pipeline is a hammer. You’ve been using it to build a TV ad. That’s why every video looks like an empty pool deck with a voiceover whispering “your people” over a chandelier. The platform is TikTok. The audience is mid-scroll. The competition is a guy doing a card trick. Nobody is waiting for your sunset over the railing.

This playbook is not a style guide. It’s a strategy with a prompt library bolted to it. The thesis is one sentence:

Sell the relief of not having to explain yourself, using the only thing your AI can actually render: hands, tables, light, and the occasional porthole.

Everything that follows is a consequence of that sentence.

FRAME
Relief, not aspiration
“Finally, a vacation where you don’t have to explain your hobby.” The hook is a complaint they already make silently.
SUBJECT
Hands, not faces
The AI can’t render people. Stop asking it to. Animate the content of the table, not the camera around it.
SHIP
Punchline, not setting
A porthole. A wake out the window. The horn over a card flip. The ship is the absurdity, not the product.
FORMAT
Text-led, ambient audio
Drop the narration. Text does the script work. Diegetic sound (dice, shuffles) plus a trending track does the rest.
01 / HOOKS
00:02:14:08

Three frames, one priority

The audience makes three different complaints. Each one is a hook.

You don’t pick a frame and stick to it. You run all three in parallel and let the algorithm sort. But one carries the most weight in this campaign — relief — because it’s the only frame that’s also a confession. Confessions stop scrolls.

01 PRIMARY · 60% of output

Relief

“Finally, a vacation where I don’t have to explain my hobby.”

  • HOOK A nobody on this ship is going to ask what i do for a living
  • HOOK B i packed three expansions and zero small talk
  • HOOK C pov: the cruise is just 70 people who already get it

WHY IT WORKS  ·  the hook is a private thought said out loud. recognition spike in 1.5s.

02 SECONDARY · 25% of output

Identity

“Group travel for board game people.”

  • HOOK A things that go in my carry-on for a board game cruise
  • HOOK B if you’ve ever played wingspan past 1am, this is for you
  • HOOK C rating board games i’m bringing on a cruise ship

WHY IT WORKS  ·  pulls the proud-niche cohort. high comment rate, lower share rate.

03 TERTIARY · 15% of output

Social

“A cruise where the people are the point.”

  • HOOK A day three and i’ve already played catan with strangers seven times
  • HOOK B the friendship group chat we made at sea
  • HOOK C nobody wanted to leave the lounge at 2am

WHY IT WORKS  ·  FOMO + parasocial. weakest first-frame hook, strongest mid-funnel.

RULE

If your hook reads like a brochure, kill it. Hooks should sound like a text from a friend who is slightly tired and slightly relieved.

02 / WHAT’S WORKING
00:05:48:21

Travel + niche-community TikTok, observed

The patterns we’re going to steal — and the ones we’re going to ignore.

STEAL ↗

POV + text-led

“POV: you finally found your people”. Text does the narrative; video is mood. Made for our pipeline.

STEAL ↗

Listicle / counting structure

“3 things nobody tells you about…” Hard hook, episodic structure, easy to template.

STEAL ↗

Diegetic ASMR ambience

Dice clatter, card shuffle, ice in a glass. No narration. The sound IS the script.

STEAL ↗

Comment-driven CTA

“Comment GAMES for the link.” Boosts comments → algo boost. Beats “link in bio” every time.

SKIP ×

Day-in-my-life

Requires a continuous character. Our AI can’t deliver. Fakes will register as fakes.

SKIP ×

Reaction / talking-head

Needs a face. We don’t have one. Stop trying.

SKIP ×

Drone / aerial reveal

Reads as ad in 0.4 seconds. Also: every cruise line has done it.

SKIP ×

Trending dance / lip-sync

Wrong audience, wrong vibe, no plausible execution.

The four formats we’ll actually ship.

  1. FMT-01 POV-TEXT First-person framing, full-bleed text overlays, ambient audio, 12–18s.
  2. FMT-02 LISTICLE “Five things on a board game cruise”. 3–4s per beat, hard cuts, 18–25s.
  3. FMT-03 ASMR-AMBIENCE No text in first 3s. Pure diegetic sound. Text resolves the tension at 6s. 15–20s.
  4. FMT-04 CONFESSION Single sustained pull-quote, slow text reveal, one image, one sound. 8–12s.

Every frame is one of these eight compositions. No exceptions.

The pipeline fails when we ask for things image-to-video can’t do: perspective changes, multiple humans in motion, faces. It succeeds when we ask for small in-frame motion in tight, content-rich crops. Memorize the green column. Reject anything in the red column at the source-image stage.
